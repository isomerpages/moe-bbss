<p></p>
<h4><strong><u>CCA Experience and Trials</u></strong></h4>
<p>The CCA Experience and Trials serve to offer the Secondary 1 students
a platform to get to know the various CCAs in BBSS, discover their strengths
and have some hands-on experience. After these trial sessions, your child/ward
will also be strongly encouraged to find out more about the CCAs of his/her
interest during Week 3 through observing the CCAs’ training sessions.</p>
<p>The CCA Experience and Trials will take place on 12 Jan &amp; 14 Jan from
2.30pm-6.00pm.</p>
<p>The students will be guided by CCA student leaders during the sessions.
Do remind your child/ward to have a proper lunch in the school canteen
before attending the CCA trials.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong><u>Seek, Discover and Learn (SDL) Day</u></strong></h4>
<p>The school has set aside time for students engage in self-directed learning
from home. Students do not need to report to school; instead, they are
to log into online platforms such as the Student Learning Space (SLS) to
complete their assignments and to use the time to pursue their own interests
and learn beyond the curriculum.</p>
<p>The Secondary 1 students will receive their Personal Learning Devices
(PLD) later in the school term. More details on the purchase of the PLD
will be shared via PG at a later date. In the meantime, the school is able
to loan out devices for students who need one for their learning at home.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong><u>Sec 1 PLD Orientation Session 1</u></strong></h4>
<p>Prior to receiving the PLD, all Secondary 1 students will be guided on
using technology appropriately and safely, while developing basic digital
competencies such as accessing the SLS and organising digital learning
resources. This will help build their confidence and prepare them for learning
in a digital environment.</p>
<p>The first session of the PLD Orientation will take place on:
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 13 Jan (Tue) 2.30pm to 3.45pm
(for 1A1, 1A2 and 1A3 students)
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 15 Jan (Thu) 2.30pm to 3.45pm
(for 1A4, 1A5, 1A6 and 1A7 students)</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The session will be conducted in the school computer labs after school.
Please remind your child/ward to have a proper lunch in the school canteen
before attending the session.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong><u>Multi-Level Activities (MLA)</u></strong></h4>
<p>The Sec 1 students will be attending a 4D3N camp from <strong>26 to 29 January 2026</strong> where

will be shared with you at a later date. Please provide details of your
child/ward's <u>medical conditions and dietary restrictions</u> (if any)

<h4><strong><u>Parents’ Support Group</u></strong></h4>
<p>BBSS is proud to have a dedicated Parents’ Support Group (PSG) that plays
an active role in supporting the holistic development of our students.
The PSG serves as a valuable platform for parents to partner with the school,
fostering a supportive and engaged school community.</p>
<p>The objectives of the BBSS PSG are as follows:
<br>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To actively involve parents
in their children’s secondary school journey
<br>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To support the holistic development
of students in BBSS
<br>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To build rapport among parents
and provide mutual support through meaningful educational and social activities</p>
<p>More information about the PSG and details on how to register can be found
here:</p>

<h4><strong><u>Appeals</u></strong></h4>
<p>Students seeking transfer may complete this&nbsp;<a href="https://form.gov.sg/5f96453b2464570011d2fee5" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">online transfer form</a>,
with softcopy supporting documents.</p>
<p>We review every application carefully, taking a holistic view in determining
suitability for admission, considering academic results, passion/interests,
reason for transfer, etc. As the application is subject to available vacancies,
please note that admission is not guaranteed.</p>
<p>Do note that your child's PSLE score should meet the school's cut-off
point (COP). For more information, you may also refer to MOE website -&nbsp;
<a rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.moe.gov.sg/microsites/psle-fsbb/posting-to-secondary-school/faqs.html</a>
</p>
